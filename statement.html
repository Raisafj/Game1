Anthropy argues that “The experience we call a game is created by the interactions between different rules, but the rules themselves aren’t the game, the interaction is! A game can’t exist without a player or players: someone needs to be engaging with the rules for the experience to happen.” Hence, the rules are not the game but TimTim’s interaction with the rules is what makes it a game.

Artist's Statement:

Have you ever wondered what it would be like if the non-living things could express how they feel! Timtim’s first date is a playful art piece where the player helps Timtim the main character of the game to reach his date, Cece. Our main goal was to show a romantic yet an adventurous day in a non-living thing’s life.

Like every other day Timtim was sleeping forgetting he has a date . Cece’s texts wakes him up and he rushes his way out. As he rushes, he breaks his glass so the players need to help him remember his things from his memory as he cannot see without his glasses. In closet.html player needs to match each pair to get Timtim ready for his date. In page findCar.html Timtim walks towards his car. He needs to drive safely without hitting 1500 miles safely without hitting another car in carGame.html to reach the next stop. On his way he remembered he forgot flowers. He looks for a garden in findGarden.html and collects flowers for Cece in flowers.html.
