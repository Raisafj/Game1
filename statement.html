Anthropy argues that “The experience we call a game is created by the interactions between different rules, but the rules themselves aren’t the game, the interaction is! Artist's Statement:

TimTim and his first date are discussed through our work. It's an adventurous game and a playful piece of art where we follow TimTim and his very first date! TimTim did the same, much as we all made mistakes when we went on our first date. What was his mistake, then? So what is his mistake? TimTim overslept and forgot about his date and it’s on our hand to help him reach Cece - his date - on time before she gets mad and leaves.

In the beginning, we see Cece texting him, concerned if he’s coming on the date or not as he’s not replying and is already. She was disappointed however, we wake him up and help him get ready but accidents don’t leave TimTim alone so soon. We move on to the next page which is closet.html where TimTim breaks his glasses due to the rush. So now, he can barely see and needs to get ready as soon as possible and the only way to get ready is by remembering where he kept his things - this introduces us to the memory game in the closet where TimTim has to find pair of each of his objects by remembering to continue. After TimTim is ready we take him to the car. Once he’s in the car we have to help TimTim drive 1500 miles safely in carGame.html to reach his next stop. Like I said before, accidents don’t leave TimTim alone so soon - midway he realizes he forgot flowers for Cece luckily he finds a garden and has to collect it in flowers.html, and only then he can reach his date, Cece. We have also added a map for players to enjoy the games individually without playing as the series we planned. They can skip to each of these games - cargame.html, closet.html, and flowers.html - and moreover helps the players to navigate.

Our goal was to have an adventurous game and take the users on a TimTim’s journey. We wanted to have a story within the game. It is light-hearted, playful, and slightly romantic for kids and teenagers to play. Our inspiration for this game was “Purble place” that we believe almost every Windows desktop user has come across. Purble Place is a collection of single-player, strategy games which are developed by Oberon Games.
